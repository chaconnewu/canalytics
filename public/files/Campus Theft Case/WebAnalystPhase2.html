Role Description:

Your role as a Web Analyst is to analyze the evidences that you found from Internet activities.  Examples of this type of information are evidences from Facebook, twitter, online market places, or email correspondences. This type of information is more likely to help you find out the social relationships of the suspects, which can be your unique contribution to the group discussion.

The police have verified all the social relationships mentioned in the document.  They have also verified that when persons of interest had classes or activities, they actually attended and stayed until the classes or activities ended.  However, they might have a chance to sneak out for a 5- to 10- minute break depended on the types of classes or activities.

In this task, each team member holds unique information relevant to the case.  To solve the case, it is imperative to share the information you have with your teammates.   

 

 

The time is 4:15pm on Saturday Nov 14, 2015.  Based on the list of eight people that your team provided yesterday, the investigation team spent a day collected the intelligence included in this packet.  Today, the goals of your team are to use all the information you have (include the information given in phase 1) to:

 

1.     Determine the thief/thieves for each of the four laptop thefts

2.     Determine whether the thieves stole for their own purpose or if there was an instigator (i.e., the person who encourages or stimulates another person to commit crime) behind each theft. If so, specify who.  (Note: The instigator(s) may not be in the list of 8 people that you narrowed down to) 

3.     Specify possible motives for each of the four thefts.

4.     Specify any possible connections between the thefts.

 

Again, you need to give your answers to the investigation team by the end of today (5pm) so they can start their investigation as soon as tomorrow begins and focus their investigation on only critical people involved.  This leaves your team 45 minutes to analyze the intelligence. 

 

Note that the intelligence provided to you and your teammates are not overlapping.  Thus, collaborating with your teammates is essential to solve the questions above.

 

Write down your answers to each of the questions on the answer sheet provided before time is up.
