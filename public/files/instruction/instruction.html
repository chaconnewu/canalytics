Case

A fire broke out at 422 E. College Avenue around 5:50pm on Thursday July 17, 2014. Jason Tully, the owner of the house, fell asleep while cooking. The fire was not noticed until Jason’s daughter, Emily Tully, came back home after school. She typically goes back home for dinner before her piano class every Thursday night. She called the fire company immediately. Fortunately, located at 400 W Beaver Ave, the Alpha Fire Company is quite close and arrived early enough to get the fire under control.

Instruction

Please get familiar with the tool by completing the following tasks:

  • In the Case section above, select and annotate the sentence “A fire broke out at … July 17, 2014”. Annotate the event ‘fire break’, add location and time (note you can also annotate in the calendar tool). Then open the calendar to see the event you just created on July 17, 2014.
  • In the Case section above, select and annotate the phrase “Jason’s dayghter, Emily Tully”. Annotate the relation between Jason Tully and Emily Tully. Then open the graph tool and see the relation graph you just created! Hover over the link to see the detailed information.
  • Open the calendar tool, and create events to annotate Emily’s routine piano class every Thursday night (remember you have to provide an exact time so that the system can save the date, say 8–10pm).
  • In the Case section above, select and annotate the sentence “located at 400…the Alpha Fire Company”. Annotate the location of the fire company. Then open the map tool and see the two places you just created!
  • Open Notepad tool, and type whatever your insight or intermediate thoughts. Your teammates can see what you type in real time!
  • Try out filtering functions on the right side bar of each tool

Note

By the time of the experiment, the system is not yet perfect. There are a few things to keep in mind:

  • When inputting date, please provide the full date and time (including year, month, day, hour, minute, second, and am/pm).
  • When inputting location, if the location cannot be found, try using the address in the parenthesis instead of the place name. For buildings in Penn State, put the building name followed by 'Penn State' may help you better locate the place. For example, put 'hub penn state' instead of 'hub' only.
